In-Play Match Reasoning

02 / Methodology

Evaluation Pipeline Design

A strict, modular architecture designed to evaluate live reasoning and prevent any future information leakage.

1 Data Slicing
Standardizes raw StatsBomb World Cup 2022 timelines. Truncates match events chronologically at precisely T = 20', 45', and 70' to isolate current game state.
2 Zero-Leakage Agent
Recalculates scores using strictly historical events (≤ T). Formats timelines and queries models at Temp = 0 for structured JSON completions.
3 Deterministic Grader
An automated rules-based engine checks predictions against actual match remaining events. Penalizes vague responses or hedging (is_gradable = 0).

04 / Calibration & Adaptability

How Well Do Models Know What They Know?

Evaluating prediction confidence calibration and sensitivity to game-changing events.

Confidence Calibration
  • Gemma-31B Excellence: Shows robust high-confidence calibration, reaching 75.0% accuracy when stating 70-100% confidence.
  • Overconfidence Control: Low-to-medium confidence bins correctly yield lower accuracies, proving self-awareness.
Update Test (Sensitivity)
  • Qwen-27B Perfection: Achieved 100% consistency ratio, shifting its win probability vector in the correct direction on every single goal/red card.
  • Gemma-31B Consistency: Logged a strong 80% consistency ratio across match transitions.
05 / Takeaways

Conclusions & Future Horizons

  • Contextual Reasoning: Models successfully synthesize momentum, cards, and substitutions, going beyond pure statistical scorelines.
  • Complementary Capabilities: Qwen is highly sensitive to live events (100% reactivity), while Gemma is better calibrated in its final assertions.
  • Practical Value: Proves LLMs can act as real-time sports analysts or risk engines.
Future Work
  • Consensus Multi-Agent Systems (cooperation of Qwen + Gemma).
  • Integrating multimodal inputs (visual broadcasts).
  • Testing on larger datasets (1000+ matches).
